    Newton's Laws of Motion

    Newton’s three laws of motion form the backbone of classical mechanics. These laws explain how forces influence the motion of objects.

    1. First Law – Law of Inertia

    An object remains at rest or moves in a straight line at constant speed unless acted upon by a net external force.

    2. Second Law – F = ma

    The net force on an object is equal to the mass of the object multiplied by its acceleration: F = ma.

    3. Third Law – Action and Reaction

    For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

    Wave Theory

    Wave theory describes how energy travels through oscillations. There are two main types of waves: transverse and longitudinal.

    • Wavelength – distance between two crests or troughs
    • Frequency – how many waves pass a point per second
    • Speed – wave speed = frequency × wavelength

    Electricity

    Electricity is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. It involves concepts like current, voltage, resistance, and power.

    • Current (I) – flow of charge
    • Voltage (V) – potential difference
    • Ohm’s Law: V = IR

    Magnetism

    Magnetism refers to the force exerted by magnets when they attract or repel each other.

    • Magnetic fields are created by moving charges.
    • Like poles repel, unlike poles attract.
    • Magnetic field lines go from north to south.
